## Partial Convergence Cases

### Case 26: Sukkah Height (10 Tefachim)

- **Analysis**: Both frameworks agree on the validity threshold of 10 tefachim for a kosher sukkah (Sukkah 29b-30a). The Maharal's approach emphasizes the purposive form requiring minimal dwelling space, while the Lurianic analysis focuses on the sefirot channel dimensions. Agreement exists on the threshold itself, but divergence occurs on why specifically 10 tefachim rather than 7 or 12.

### Case 27: Four Species (Arba Minim)

- **Analysis**: Agreement on the necessity of all four species, but partial divergence on the numerical significance of specifically four species versus other potential combinations.

### Case 28: Kezayit Volume

- **Analysis**: Frameworks converge on the minimum volume requirement, but differ on the underlying rationale for this specific measure.

### Case 29: Sefirat HaOmer (49 Days)

- **Analysis**: Full agreement on the 49-day count from Pesach to Shavuot, but divergence on whether the significance derives from purposive completion (Maharal) or sefirot channel alignment (Lurianic).

### Case 30: Minyan (10 Person Threshold)

- **Analysis**: Both systems validate the requirement of 10 men for communal prayer, but differ on whether this derives from essential communal form (Maharal) or divine light channeling requirements (Lurianic).

## Overall Framework Analysis

### Limitations
