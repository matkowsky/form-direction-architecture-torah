## I. The Epistemology of Measurement

### 1.1 What Is Measurement?

**Standard Definition (Physics)**: Measurement is the assignment of numerical values to properties according to a rule.

**Key Insight**: *All* measurement involves theoretical interpretation:
- Temperature: We don't measure "heat" directly; we measure mercury expansion and *interpret* it as temperature
- Mass: We measure gravitational effects and *interpret* them as mass
- Time: We measure periodic phenomena (pendulums, atomic oscillations) and *interpret* them as time

**Implication**: The objection "you cannot measure spiritual alignment in a laboratory" misunderstands measurement. We never measure phenomena directly—we always measure *indicators* and interpret their significance.

### 1.2 Operational Definitions in Science

**Bridgman's Operationalism**: A concept is defined by the operations used to measure it.

- Temperature: Defined operationally by thermometer readings

2. **Intention Alignment I(s_a, s_b)**: Defined by directional correlation
   - *Operation*: Analyze textual statements about kavannah (intention)

   - *Operation*: Examine outcomes in halakhic rulings that involve consciousness

**Defense**: Our measurements are *operationally defined*, exactly as in empirical science. The operations are textual analysis rather than laboratory experiments, but the methodological structure is identical.
