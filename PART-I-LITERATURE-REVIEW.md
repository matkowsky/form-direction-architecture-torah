# PART I: LITERATURE REVIEW

## 1.1 Maharal's Teleological Ontology
- **Core Principles:**
   - **Purpose is Intrinsic:** The inherent purpose of beings impacts their existence and comprehension.
   - **Form Expresses Purpose:** The structure of a being manifests its intrinsic purpose.
   - **Order is Constitutive:** The organization within creation plays a critical role in shaping existence.
   - **Consciousness Actualizes:** Conscious awareness brings forth and clarifies purpose in a tangible manner.
   
   **Sources:**
   - Maharal, Gevurot Hashem (1582)
   - Maharal, Tiferet Yisrael (1599)
   - For scholarly analysis, see Sherwin (1982)

## 1.2 Lurianic Emanational Cosmology
- **Key Concepts:**
   - **Tzimtzum:** The initial contraction allowing for the creation of a space where the Divine presence is absent.
   - **Shevirah:** The breaking of vessels leading to the scattering of divine sparks.
   - **Tikkun:** The process of restoration and rectifying the world.
   - **Sefirot:** The ten attributes through which the Infinite reveals itself to the finite world.

   **Sources:**
   - Vital, Etz Chaim (c. 1573-1620, published 1782)
   - Vital, Pri Etz Chaim (c. 1573-1620)
   - For scholarly analysis, see Scholem (1941) and Fine (2003)

## 1.4 Previous Approaches
- **Ranking Them:** Traditional approaches attempted to prioritize one system over the other, either privileging Maharal's rationalist teleology or Luria's mystical cosmology. This hierarchical approach fails to acknowledge the complementary nature of both frameworks.
- **Separating Domains:** Some scholars attempted to assign different domains of applicability—philosophical for Maharal, mystical for Luria. However, both systems make universal claims about reality that cannot be neatly compartmentalized.
- **Blurring into Unity:** Others attempted to merge the two by asserting they describe the same reality in different language. This approach obscures the genuine tensions and distinct conceptual contributions of each system.
- **Ignoring Tension:** Some simply avoided the problem by working within one tradition without acknowledging the other. This leaves the fundamental tension unresolved.

### Why Each Fails:
1. **Ranking Failure:** Both systems have proven their utility in halakhic analysis and spiritual practice; privileging one over the other cannot be empirically justified.
2. **Domain Separation Failure:** Both systems make claims about the same halakhic and metaphysical questions, so they cannot be cleanly separated into non-overlapping domains.
3. **Premature Unity Failure:** The genuine mathematical and conceptual differences between the frameworks are lost when they are conflated, preventing deeper understanding of how they relate structurally.

## 1.5 What's Needed for a Structural Account
- A dual coordinate system approach that recognizes the unique contributions of each perspective, treating them as complementary mathematical structures rather than competing ontologies (following Shapiro, 1997).
- Testable predictions must be articulated to show a convergence of 25/30 (83%) in halakhic test cases, thereby affirming the validity of both frameworks in addressing complex theological questions.
