# PART II: MATHEMATICAL FOUNDATIONS

## Methodological Note
The use of mathematical formalism in this work follows in the tradition of the Vilna Gaon and other Torah scholars who recognized that mathematics can serve as a language to describe relationships and structures, without reducing Torah concepts to mere mathematical objects. The Hilbert space framework is employed as an **analogy and analytical tool**, not as a claim that spiritual realities are literally mathematical constructs. As the Rambam wrote in *Moreh Nevuchim*, abstract language can help us grasp relationships between concepts that transcend literal description.

## 2.5 Idolatry as Orthogonal Subspace
We explore the concept of idolatry (*avodah zarah*) as an Orthogonal Subspace H_orth. In this framework, idolatry represents a complete misalignment with divine purpose - not merely a partial deviation, but an orientation that is perpendicular (orthogonal) to the proper direction of consciousness and action. This mathematical representation captures the halakhic principle that idolatry is categorically distinct from other transgressions, existing in a fundamentally opposed relationship to kedushah (holiness).
