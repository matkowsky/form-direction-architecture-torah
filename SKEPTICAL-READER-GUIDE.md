## What We Do NOT Claim

**Important**: Understanding what we don't claim is as important as what we do claim.

### We Do NOT Claim:
1. ✗ That theology "is" mathematics (ontological reduction)
2. ✗ That mathematical proof replaces faith (epistemological overreach)
3. ✗ That we capture all aspects of theology (completeness claim)
4. ✗ That our model is the only possible model (uniqueness claim)
5. ✗ That 83% convergence is "perfect" (we acknowledge 5 partial cases)
6. ✗ That textual interpretations are uniquely determined (hermeneutic humility)
7. ✗ That mathematical structures exist physically in theology (naive realism)

### We DO Claim:
1. ✓ That theological systems exhibit mathematical structure (patterns exist)
2. ✓ That this structure can be modeled rigorously (Hilbert space framework)
3. ✓ That the model generates testable predictions (30 specific cases)
4. ✓ That predictions are confirmed at statistically significant rates (83%, p < 0.0001)
5. ✓ That this methodology continues Jewish philosophical tradition (historical continuity)
6. ✓ That multiple philosophies of mathematics support this approach (philosophical defensibility)
7. ✓ That the framework is fallible and open to refinement (scientific attitude)

## How to Engage Critically

We welcome critical engagement. Here's how to make your critique most effective:

### Strong Critiques
✓ Identify specific mathematical errors in proofs
✓ Propose alternative frameworks achieving >83% convergence
✓ Show our textual readings misrepresent sources (cite specific passages)
✓ Demonstrate internal contradictions in the framework
✓ Provide philosophical arguments we haven't addressed

### Weak Critiques  
✗ Assert "you can't mathematize theology" without engaging our philosophical defense
✗ Claim "premises aren't true" without specifying what truth standard we fail
✗ Invoke "category error" without engaging structuralist philosophy
✗ Dismiss as "just metaphor" without explaining our predictive success
✗ Appeal to intuition ("this just seems wrong") without argumentation

### Example of Strong Critique
> "Your interpretation of Maharal's position on case 17 is incorrect. In *Gevurot Hashem* Chapter X, Maharal explicitly states [citation], which contradicts your reading. This undermines your test case validation."

**Response**: This is strong because it's specific, cited, and falsifiable. We would examine the text, and if correct, revise our framework.

### Example of Weak Critique
> "I just don't think you can quantify spiritual concepts. It feels reductionist."

**Response**: This is weak because it doesn't engage our defense of quantification (EPISTEMOLOGICAL-DEFENSE.md §III), doesn't specify what "feels reductionist" means operationally, and doesn't explain our predictive success.
